Why Q-Tips Are Making Your Ears Worse

"The problem is that most ear-cleaning methods can't actually remove earwax. "Cotton swabs don't extract wax from the ear canal," explains Dr. Ordon. "They compress it. “Every time you insert a cotton swab into your ear, you’re not removing wax. You’re compacting it — layer by layer, closer to the eardrum.”

The American Academy of Otolaryngology estimates that over 35 million Americans are currently affected by excess or impacted earwax. Harvard Medical School identifies impacted earwax as the primary cause of conductive hearing loss - the kind that gets misdiagnosed for years as age-related decline.

"We now have research connecting chronic hearing loss - including the kind caused by wax buildup - to accelerated cognitive decline and increased dementia risk. When your brain works overtime to process muffled sound, year after year, it's a brain under chronic stress."

The Lancet Commission on Dementia (2020) identified hearing loss as one of the most significant modifiable risk factors for dementia - ahead of smoking, physical inactivity, and depression.
