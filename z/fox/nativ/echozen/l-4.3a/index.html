Tinnitus: What Scientific Research Reveals

Informational article · Based on scientific research

Tinnitus is the perception of sound — such as ringing, buzzing, hissing, or clicking — without an external source. It is estimated that approximately 15% of the global population experiences some degree of tinnitus during their lifetime.

According to the National Institute on Deafness and Other Communication Disorders (NIDCD), tinnitus may be associated with prolonged exposure to loud noise, age-related hearing loss, earwax buildup, or changes in blood circulation near the inner ear.

Scientific understanding of the neurological mechanisms behind tinnitus has advanced considerably in recent years. Access the full NIDCD resource to learn about the latest research on causes, risk factors, and evidence-based management approaches for this condition.
